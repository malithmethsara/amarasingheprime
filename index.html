Explanation of Vehicle Tax Types

Customs Import Duty (CID)

Customs Import Duty is the foundational tax applied to the import of goods. For motor vehicles, it is calculated as a percentage of the CIF (Cost, Insurance, and Freight) value. This is the first tax levied by Sri Lanka Customs.

  • Relevant Gazette: The rates and regulations for CID are periodically updated. Refer to the latest Sri Lanka Customs Tariff Guide for the most current information.

Surcharge

A Surcharge is an additional levy imposed on top of the Customs Import Duty. It is often a temporary measure introduced by the government to manage foreign exchange reserves and control imports. This is a crucial component that significantly increases the final tax amount.

  • Relevant Gazette: The surcharge was introduced via Gazette No. 2421/43 dated 31.01.2025.

Excise Duty

Excise Duty is a specific tax on the import of vehicles, with rates varying based on the vehicle's engine capacity (in CC) or motor power (in kW), fuel type, and age. This tax component is the primary determinant of a vehicle's final price, especially for higher-engine capacity vehicles.

  • Relevant Gazette: The most recent rates are governed by Gazette Notification No. 2418/43 dated 10.01.2025.

Luxury Tax

The Luxury Tax is applied to vehicles whose CIF value exceeds a specific threshold. This tax aims to discourage the import of high-value vehicles. The threshold and the tax rate depend on the vehicle's fuel type.

  • Relevant Gazette: Details on the Luxury Tax are available in Gazette Notification No. 2421/41 dated 31.01.2025.

VAT (Value Added Tax)

VAT is a consumption tax that is applied at the final stage of the import process. It is calculated on the total value of the vehicle after all other taxes (CID, Surcharge, Excise, and Luxury Tax) have been applied. The current VAT rate is 18%.

  • Relevant Gazette: The VAT regulations are outlined by the Inland Revenue Department. Refer to IRD publications for more details.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the CIF value?
CIF stands for Cost, Insurance, and Freight. It is the total value of the vehicle up to the point it arrives at the port in Sri Lanka, including the cost of the vehicle itself, shipping insurance, and freight charges.
Why is my calculated tax different from a professional quote?
Our calculator provides an estimate based on the main tax components. The final tax can be affected by additional levies like Port and Airport Levy (PAL) and Social Security Contribution Levy (SSCL) as well as specific vehicle classifications and ongoing regulatory changes. Always consult with a licensed customs agent for an exact quote.
Are there age limits for imported vehicles?
Yes, there are age restrictions on imported vehicles. Passenger cars must be less than three years old from the date of manufacture. Other vehicle types like three-wheelers and goods vehicles have different age limits.
How often do vehicle tax laws change in Sri Lanka?
Vehicle tax laws and import regulations in Sri Lanka are subject to change, often influenced by the country's economic policies and foreign exchange situation. It is important to refer to the official gazettes published by the Ministry of Finance and Sri Lanka Customs for the most up-to-date information.
