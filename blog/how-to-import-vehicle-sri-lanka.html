How to Import a Vehicle to Sri Lanka (2025 Step-by-Step)

Importing a vehicle from Japan to Sri Lanka is often cheaper than buying locally, but it can be intimidating if you don't know the process. With the 2025 import restrictions lifted, many are rushing to open Letters of Credit (LCs).

Don't make rookie mistakes. Here is the exact 5-step process we use at Amarasinghe Prime to get vehicles safely to your doorstep.

Step 1: The Auction Selection

Unlike local car sales where you see the polished product, buying from Japan gives you access to the Auction Sheet. This is the vehicle's "Medical Report".

Pro Tip: Never buy a vehicle without translating the auction sheet notes. Look for "Grade 4.5" or higher for mint condition. Avoid "R" grade unless you are an expert on accident repairs.

Step 2: Calculate the "Real" Cost

The CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight) price you see in Japan is just the beginning. You need to calculate the landed cost including Sri Lankan taxes.

In 2025, you must account for:

  • Customs Import Duty (CID)
  • Excise Duty (Based on Engine Capacity)
  • Luxury Tax (If CIF > 5M LKR)
  • PAL & VAT: Often missed in rough calculations!

If you get this math wrong, your car might get stuck at the Hambantota or Colombo port.

Step 3: Opening the LC (Letter of Credit)

You cannot simply bank transfer money to Japan. You must open an LC through a recognized local bank. To do this, you will need:

  1. A Performa Invoice from the Japanese supplier.
  2. Your Tax Identification Number (TIN).
  3. Budget for the bank's LC opening charges.

Step 4: Shipping & Documents

Once the LC is processed and the supplier is paid, the vehicle is booked on a vessel (RORO Shipping). The supplier will send the original documents (BL - Bill of Lading, Export Certificate) to your bank.

Step 5: Clearing & Registration

When the ship arrives, you need a Customs House Agent (CHA) to clear the goods. They will submit the "CusDec" (Customs Declaration) and pay the taxes we calculated in Step 2.

After clearing, the final step is obtaining your RMV Registration (Number Plate). This usually takes 1-2 days after the vehicle passes the RMV inspection.
